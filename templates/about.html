settings

An activity that yields a high number of leads, might not provide you with the best return on your investment if those leads do not convert into sales.

It is important in strategic marketing to plan your spend based on what will provide you the best return on investment. Having a clear overview of your campaigns will help you achieve this.

FAQs

  • question_answer How much does an account cost?

    Nothing! We are a small start-up and at the moment the service is completely free!

  • question_answer Is my company data secure?

    Yes, completely. The data is kept in a secure database on another server, linked to the website. All data entered into the site is used for the Benchmark data shown. It is an average per industry and company details are never displayed.

  • question_answer My marketing team works across multiple industries and countries; how is this managed?

    The site has been developed for small-medium enterprises who are less likely to work across multiple industries and/or countries, therefore at the moment account profiles are one industry/country only; it is not possible to have multiple industries or countries per account.

    The best way to monitor multiple industries and/or countries is by having one account for each.

  • question_answer How do you differentiate between marketing, sales, and converted leads?

    This depends on your business model, however we suggest you use the following basic guidelines:

    Marketing leads are initial enquiries, that need more information, or communication before they convert into a sales lead.

    A sales lead is a more positive enquiry that has a higher possibility of converting into business.

    A converted lead is where the sales lead has turned into a sale and money has exchanged hands.

    Take into account that the flow of leads from marketing, through sales into a transaction is not linear and will depend very much on the business, industry and whether you are B2B or B2C for example.
